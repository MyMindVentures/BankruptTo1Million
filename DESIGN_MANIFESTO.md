# Bankrupt to 1 Million — Visual Design Manifesto

## Purpose

The platform must express the mission: rebuilding from rock bottom through intelligence, resilience, community and visible progress.

The design should never suggest poverty, desperation or a lack of ambition. It should communicate resourcefulness, discipline and Proof of Mind: limited resources combined with strong thinking, clear intent and exceptional execution.

## Core Direction

The visual narrative moves through six stages:

**Rock bottom → first light → human connection → shared momentum → tangible progress → freedom**

The experience should feel like a premium independent documentary, not a startup template, charity campaign, luxury brand or get-rich-quick platform.

## Design Principles

### 1. Intelligence before decoration

Every visual decision must have a clear purpose. Quality comes from hierarchy, composition, typography, photography, rhythm and restraint—not from unnecessary effects.

### 2. Dignity before pity

Hardship may be shown honestly, but never exploited. The platform presents capable people rebuilding with talent, willpower and community support.

### 3. People before products

Kevin, Micha, hosts, builders, creators and early supporters are the central subjects. Technology supports the story; it is not the story.

### 4. Evidence before claims

Show real issues, commits, contributors, milestones, stories and progress. Never invent traction, partners, testimonials, statistics or success.

### 5. Premium without luxury signalling

The website must feel valuable without relying on wealth imagery. Avoid visual clichés associated with millionaire brands, crypto, hustle culture or artificial success.

### 6. Documentary rather than template-driven

Pages should read as chapters in a living story. Use asymmetrical editorial compositions, strong pacing, meaningful whitespace and authentic photography.

### 7. Recognition without gamification

Founding contributors should receive permanent, dignified recognition. Profiles must feel like editorial portraits—not badges, rankings or leaderboard entries.

### 8. Consistency over personal preference

Contributors must follow approved tokens, components, layouts and issue references. A small feature may not introduce a new visual language or redesign unrelated work.

## Visual Language

### Colour

- **Rock Bottom Black — `#0A0A0B`**: primary background
- **Charcoal — `#151517`**: cards and secondary surfaces
- **Warm Ivory — `#F2EEE6`**: primary light text and selected light sections
- **Stone — `#B8B1A7`**: metadata, quiet copy and borders
- **Founding Gold — `#C89B5B`**: primary CTA and recognition accent
- **Ember — `#A65F46`**: human warmth and selected story moments
- **Deep Olive — `#4D5646`**: grounded progress and community themes
- **Muted Blue — `#4E6879`**: trust and neutral information

Gold must remain restrained. It represents belief and momentum, not luxury.

### Typography

- **Display:** Cormorant Garamond
- **UI and body:** Manrope

Use serif typography for emotion, memory and major statements. Use sans-serif typography for clarity, actions, forms and structured information.

Maintain one dominant headline per viewport, readable line lengths and a minimum body size of 16 px on mobile.

### Photography

Prefer real people, real work, natural environments, conversations, shared meals, travel, practical contribution and visible progress.

Use natural light, honest expressions, restrained grading and thoughtful crops. Early story sections may feel darker and more intimate; community and progress sections may become warmer and more open.

Never use staged poverty, fake luxury, generic handshakes, AI-looking people, coworking stock photography, victory poses or imagery that implies guaranteed wealth.

### Layout

Use a documentary rhythm:

**Image → story → evidence → action**

Prefer asymmetric editorial splits, varied image scale, strong negative space and one focal point per section. Avoid repetitive three-card layouts and symmetrical template patterns.

### Motion

Motion must be subtle and functional:

- short fades
- small vertical reveals
- restrained image scaling
- clear hover and focus feedback

Respect reduced-motion preferences. Avoid bouncing, spinning, scroll hijacking, excessive parallax and celebratory effects.

## Components

All components must:

- use semantic HTML;
- reuse approved design tokens;
- support mobile, keyboard navigation and long translated text;
- include hover, focus, active, disabled, loading and error states where relevant;
- keep content separate from presentation;
- avoid unnecessary dependencies.

Founding Hero profiles must support public and anonymous recognition without reducing dignity.

## Explicitly Avoid

- generic SaaS or AI-generated landing-page aesthetics;
- neon blue or purple gradients;
- excessive glassmorphism;
- fake dashboards or decorative metrics;
- glossy black-and-gold millionaire styling;
- luxury-flex or hustle-culture imagery;
- abstract AI robots and brains;
- crowded card walls;
- invented social proof;
- aggressive donation or volunteer prompts;
- arbitrary redesigns by individual contributors.

## Pull Request Design Check

Before approval, confirm:

- the feature strengthens the mission and story;
- the result feels human, intentional and credible;
- approved tokens and components are reused;
- the design does not resemble a generic AI template;
- mobile, keyboard and reduced-motion behavior were tested;
- no unapproved claims, profiles or social proof were introduced;
- the contributor stayed within the assigned issue.

## Final Standard

The platform should not impress because it appears expensive. It should impress because every decision reveals intelligence, authenticity and intent.

**We are showing how a million-euro mission is built: one honest contribution, one thoughtful decision and one focused issue at a time.**
